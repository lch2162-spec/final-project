## Research Question
To what extent does wealth concentration among the top 1% of U.S. households determine the structure of U.S. capital markets — and how does this concentration respond to economic shocks?

## Hypothesis
If the top 1% holds a disproportionate and growing share of corporate equities, and if their portfolios are dominated by financial instruments rather than real estate, then wealth concentration is not merely a distributional outcome — it is a structural driver of capital market behavior.

## Datasets
- Federal Reserve Distributional Financial Accounts (DFA) via FRED: https://fred.stlouisfed.org/release?rid=453
- SEI Emissions Inequality Dashboard: https://emissions-inequality.org
